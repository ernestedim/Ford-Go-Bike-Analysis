Part II - Eploratory Visualization of Ford Go-Bike Dataset¶

by (Ernest Edim)¶

Investigation Overview¶

  • In my exploration of the Ford Go-Bike dataset, I worked on distribution of most numeric columns like ride period etc. My investigation showed that trip duration followed a normal distribution. Also, the distribution of users ages to understand the age groups that patronized the Bike company most. I performed some relationship analysis ride periods (in minutes) and member ages, it showed that users above 40 years of age experienced a continuous decline in the time spent riding bikes. The full details of my exploration will be covered in the following slides.

Dataset Overview¶

  • Ford Go-Bike dataset from 2019 covers details of individual rides made in the bike-sharing system comprised by mostly the San Francisco Bay area. Many other cities are covered by the complete dataset. There are data for previous years as well, but I will be working on San Francisco. The data comprises 16 columns namely: duration_sec, start_time, end_time, start_station_id, start_station_name, start_station_latitude, start_station_longitude, end_station_id, end_station_name, end_station_latitude, end_station_longitude, bike_id, user_type, member_birth_year, member_gender, bike_share_for_all_trip

Distribution of Trip Duration (in minutes)¶

The trip duration took between 1 minute and over 1400 minutes. Outliers between 120 minutes to over 1400 were removed from the dataset and then plotted on a histogram after I converted it to the logarithm scale. The distribution took a perfect normal distribution, showing that most trip duration lasted between 5 and 20 minutes.

Distribution of Member Ages¶

The plot shows that more than 80% of users are between the ages of 25 and 40 years. Only a few of them are aged 60 and above. Outliers of 80to 140 years of age were removed from the data.

Relationship between Trip duration and member ages.¶

The log-transformed Scatter plot of ride period (in minutes) against member ages shows that users aged between 20 and 40 have the highest ride periods running into over 40 minutes for some rides. Users above 40 years experience a continuous decline in their ride period to say 4 to 10 minutes.

Average trip duration per user type according to days of the week.¶

The average trip duration per user type according to days of the week plotted on a heatmap shows that the highest average duration of trips for both customer and subscriber user types is during weekends (Saturdays and Sundays). The average ride period (in minutes) for all user categories is during weekends (Saturdays and Sundays)
